About Anthony

The Mission

Anthony Mastronardi's story is one of resilience, faith, and the power of community. In December 2016, while playing in a high school hockey game for Macomb Dakota, Anthony suffered a devastating spinal cord injury that left him paralyzed from the neck down after crashing into the boards. What followed was a long and difficult road filled with surgeries, rehabilitation, and the challenge of learning how to live with a life-changing injury.

Despite the obstacles, Anthony approached recovery the same way he approached hockey — with determination, patience, and relentless effort. Through months of intensive therapy, he worked tirelessly to regain strength and independence while staying positive and focused on progress.

Anthony's journey quickly became bigger than one person. The hockey community rallied around him in extraordinary ways, organizing fundraising events, charity games, and support efforts that brought together teammates, families, coaches, and fans from across Michigan.

Today, Anthony's story represents more than an injury — it represents perseverance, hope, and the incredible impact of a community that refuses to let one of its own fight alone.
